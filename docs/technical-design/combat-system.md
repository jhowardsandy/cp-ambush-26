# Combat System

The combat slice supports data-defined `Direct` and `Area` delivery. Direct `Attack` names an opposing `TargetUnitId`; area `Attack` names a `TargetPosition` map tile. At completion, direct fire uses target observation/range/line-of-sight, while area delivery validates range and optional line-of-sight to its target tile then resolves active opposing units in the inclusive Manhattan radius in stable order. A legal attempt draws one seeded 1–100 value and uses the existing cover/armor vitality calculation for each affected target. `AttackResolved` exposes structured attacker/target positions and a human-readable result breakdown for presentation/replay; an illegal-at-resolution attack emits `ActionFailed`.

The starter service rifle and marksman rifle currently use provisional 75% and 85% accuracy values. Profiles default to 100% when the content omits accuracy, which keeps teaching fixtures deterministic. An attack profile may separately name ammunition and a positive ammunition cost; the resolver reserves planned attack/overwatch ammo and consumes it only for a legal direct-fire, legal area, or triggered reaction attempt, independently of hit/miss. On a hit, the generic non-negative unit armor stat applies after target-tile cover: `max(1, base damage − cover − armor)`. Area attacks do not arm overwatch and do not affect friendlies in this first delivery contract. Cover, reaction fire, ammunition, armor, and basic area delivery are implemented; evasion, facing, projectile travel, complex blast shapes, and concealment modifiers are deferred. `Aim` remains timing-only. See `../rulebook/direct-attacks.md` and `effect-system.md`.
